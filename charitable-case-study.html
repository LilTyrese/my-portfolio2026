The Trust Problem

People want to support local community causes, but they often back away right on the payment screen. Why? Because donation apps rarely show where the money actually goes. Donors are tired of vague promises; they want to see how their $25 contribution helps. When a payment path masks bank processing fees, users get suspicious and close the app.

User Interviews

I interviewed donors to understand what causes them to hesitate. The research proved that donor trust drops when financial numbers are kept unclear:

Donor Profile A

The Skeptical Giver

Wants clear proof. They hesitate to give to giant non-profits because they worry company overhead costs swallow their money.

"I want to see exactly how my cash fixes things. If it just goes into a massive corporate bank pool, I won't give."
Donor Profile B

The Express Giver

Driven by instant urgency. They want to give immediately during local crises, but quit if forced to register a profile password.

"If I see a local crisis, I want to send help in three seconds flat. Do not make me sign up for a profile password layout."

Evaluating the Market

To see how other apps handle safety, I audited three major alternatives:

Alternative Systems What They Do Well Where They Fail
GoFundMe Incredibly powerful for personal emergencies and fast sharing. Lacks long-term transparency updates. Fees can feel hidden.
Charity Navigator Excellent rating system for tax data and operational costs. Strictly informational; does not offer a fast direct payment option.
Direct Mutual Aid Links Completely direct through Venmo or cash tags. Zero built-in verification or security checks for skeptical donors.

Testing & Iterative Layout Adjustments

Testing my initial wireframe flows with real donors forced me to make quick layout updates to keep user checkouts smooth:

⚠️ User Friction

Confusing Overhead Percentages

Users felt confused by abstract percentage pie charts explaining management costs. They assumed facts were hidden.

[ 📸 Image: Abstract Pie Chart Wireframes ]
✅ The UI Fix

Plain Money Receipts

Replaced charts with a familiar cash layout breaking down exact dollars (e.g., "$22.50 to food boxes, $2.50 to credit processing").

[ 📸 Image: Clean Dollar Receipt Layout ]
⚠️ User Friction

Hidden Trust Indicators

Placing security verification seals at the bottom of forms meant users felt anxious before pressing the submit button.

[ 📸 Image: Badges Buried at Bottom ]
✅ The UI Fix

Upfront Verification Header

Moved security checkmarks and validation badges to the absolute top header so users feel safe before initiating payment steps.

[ 📸 Image: Top Header Security Badges ]

Visual Architecture

The layout uses warm, stable forest green tones (`#2B5E2F`) to establish structural security, combined with crisp cream backgrounds to reduce financial anxiety.
